Context

Why Generative AI for Climate?

🧪 Traditional Approach

  • Weather forecasting uses Numerical Weather Prediction (NWP) — solving physics equations on massive supercomputer grids
  • A 10-day global forecast needs hundreds of machines running for hours
  • Extremely expensive and energy-intensive
  • Resolution limited by computational power

🤖 Gen AI Approach

  • AI models learn patterns from decades of historical data instead of solving equations from scratch
  • Same forecast in under 60 seconds on a single machine
  • ~1,000× more energy efficient
  • Can generate higher-resolution, probabilistic outputs
💡
Key Insight: Gen AI doesn't replace scientists — it gives them tools that work 1,000× faster, at a fraction of the cost, with equal or better accuracy.
Application 1

🌦️ GraphCast — 10-Day Global Weather Forecast

Google DeepMind Published in Science, Nov 2023 Open Source

❓ The Problem

Traditional weather forecasting (NWP) needs 100s of supercomputer machines running for hours to produce a 10-day forecast. It's extremely expensive and energy-heavy.

🧠 How Gen AI Works Here

GraphCast is a Graph Neural Network (GNN) trained on 40 years of historical weather data (ERA5).

It represents Earth as a mesh of connected points (like a 3D spider web over the globe). Weather data flows between connected points — the model learns how patterns spread.

To forecast 10 days: it predicts 6 hours ahead, feeds that output back, and repeats — 40 times in under 60 seconds.

Application 2

🎲 GenCast & FourCastNet — Probabilistic Forecasting

Google DeepMind & NVIDIA GenCast: Nature, Dec 2024 · FourCastNet 3: 2025

❓ The Problem

A single forecast tells you one possible future. But weather is uncertain — especially beyond 5 days. Scientists need to know: what is the range of possibilities and how likely is each one?

🧠 How Gen AI Works Here

GenCast uses a Diffusion Model — same type of AI that generates images (like DALL-E). It starts from random "noise" and gradually shapes it into a physically realistic future weather state. Running it 50 times gives 50 different possible futures.

FourCastNet 3 uses Fourier Neural Operators — converts weather data into waves (like audio signals), processes them, and generates ensemble forecasts up to 90 days ahead.

🎲
What Is an "Ensemble"?
Instead of saying "it will rain," the AI says: "70% heavy rain, 20% moderate, 10% dry." This is far more useful for disaster planning, insurance, and energy grids.

🔍 CorrDiff — AI Downscaling (Zoom In on Weather)

NVIDIA Research Part of Earth-2 Platform Deployed: UAE & Israel 2024

❓ The Problem

Global weather models are "blurry" — they work at 25km resolution. That means one data point covers an area the size of a city. You cannot see local effects like sea breezes, building heat, or valley floods.

🧠 How Gen AI Works Here

CorrDiff is a Diffusion Model — same technology as AI image upscaling. It takes a coarse 25km forecast and generates realistic fine-scale detail down to 2km or even 200 meters.

Just like an AI can upscale a blurry photo into a sharp one, CorrDiff upscales a blurry weather map — adding physically realistic features like sea breezes and urban heat.

It does this 500× faster than traditional physics-based downscaling.

⛈️ StormScope — Short-Range Storm Prediction

NVIDIA Earth-2 Platform Operational Use 2024–25

❓ The Problem

For a flood or storm 2 hours away, you need immediate, local prediction — not a 10-day global model. Traditional short-range models lack the resolution or speed needed for real-time emergency response.

🧠 How Gen AI Works Here

StormScope takes live satellite and radar data as input and uses a Generative Convolutional AI to produce kilometer-resolution storm predictions for the next 0–6 hours.

The AI was trained to recognize storm formation patterns and "paint" realistic storm evolution in real-time.

Application 5

🛰️ Satellite Gap Filling & Earth Observation

NASA · ESA · Astraea Used Operationally

❓ The Problem

Satellites cannot see through thick clouds. When a cyclone or major weather system is present, satellite images have huge missing patches — exactly when scientists need the data most.

🧠 How Gen AI Works Here

Gen AI uses Image Inpainting — the same technology used to "erase" objects from photos. The AI was trained on thousands of complete + incomplete satellite images to learn how to realistically reconstruct missing patches.

NASA and ESA apply this to sea surface temperature, ice extent, soil moisture, and cloud cover maps — producing continuous, uninterrupted records essential for climate science.
